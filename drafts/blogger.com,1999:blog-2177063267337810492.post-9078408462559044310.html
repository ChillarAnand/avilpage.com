Few days back, I have seen this request to whitelist labnol from AdBlock. Previously, I have seen the same requests from MakeUseOf, DuckDuckGo and a few other sites.

I immediately whitelisted labnol. The ads on labnol are not much annoying. So I let them to be there. 

I surfed the site with ads for a few days. They just catch my attention, I realize that it is an ad and I will skip it. I didn't liked them at all & never clicked them. So what is the use of whitelisting a site(except if it has CPM ads)? So I again blocked the all the ads. This time I even blocked the the above request also.

But later I began to think about this. Most bloggers & webmasters are solely dependent on the online income. They have to earn their livelihood only from web! 

However, ads are not the best way to earn money. There are a lot of other ways. Instead of asking to whitelist, ask for a donation. Offering services & Selling their own products might also provide a good income.

I am a huge fan of labnol & following it since 2009. I am not worried to donate a few dollars once in a while but I don't like to whitelist it!

P.S: I am also a proud Adsense publisher. But I didn't liked the idea of making money at the cost of reader's attention and trust. So, I removed ads from this blog long back. There are a couple of ads on my other blogs. I will get rid of them too.
